Regional Markets - Emerging Market Currencies Hit by Geopolitical Tensions and US Inflation Pressures

Summary

  • Latin American markets faced pressure this week as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and shifting monetary policy expectations globally weighed on the Brazilian real and Mexican peso, with both currencies weakening due to safe-haven demand for the US dollar.

  • US inflation data significantly impacted other markets, leading to depreciation in the Indian rupee and Russian ruble due to expectations of monetary easing and weaker currency interventions, while the Turkish lira hit fresh record lows amid political and economic uncertainties following local elections.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real weakened past the 5.13 per USD mark in April, reaching its lowest level since early October as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East prompted a shift away from riskier emerging market currencies and respective expectation for its monetary policy. Dovish expectations for the Brazilian central bank added to the pressure on the real, while recent inflation data showed a slower-than-expected pace in Brazilian consumer prices at 3.93%, the lowest in nine months. This development fell within the upper tolerance limit set by the BCB and reinforced expectations for continued rate cuts post-June. Meanwhile, strong retail sales figures pointed to resilience in the Brazilian consumer, fostering hopes for economic traction. The Ibovespa fell by 1.18% to close at 127,400 on Friday, reflecting global risk aversion amid escalating tensions in the Middle East and uncertainty regarding future Fed interest rates. Credit-sensitive consumer discretionary stocks, including Dexco, Lojas Renner, and Magazine Luiza, were among the worst performers.



Mexico

Similarly, the Mexican peso weakened to below 16.7 per USD, retreating from the eight-year low of 16.3 experienced on April 8th. Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and shifting expectations for U.S. monetary policy added to the pressure on the peso. Domestically, signs of cooling emerged in the Mexican economy as monthly industrial production declined by 0.1%, well below the anticipated 0.3% expansion. Mexican headline inflation stood at 4.42%, underperforming market expectations of 4.5%, while core inflation dropped to 4.55%, its lowest level since May 2021. Mexico’s main stock market index, IPC Mexico, decreased by 2.63% over the week, reflecting similar concerns faced by Brazil amid geopolitical uncertainties and potential impacts on monetary policy.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.49% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, surged 2.48% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee depreciated past 83.35 per USD, following a high of 83.2 on April 9th, as the currency tracked the general weakness in Asian foreign exchange markets. Stubborn inflation in the U.S. raised hawkish bets on the Federal Reserve, resulting in a batch of dollar buying across emerging markets. The pressure on the rupee was exacerbated by expectations that the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) may avoid aggressively defending the rupee as it has done since the third quarter of 2023. Furthermore, speculation that the People’s Bank of China (PBoC) may accept weaker dollar pegs contributed to bets that Indian policymakers might respond with weaker intervention, allowing the rupee to depreciate alongside the yuan. Despite this, the BSE Index remained unchanged for the week.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble depreciated past 93.5 per USD, reaching its lowest level since late October 2023. This depreciation was influenced by a stronger global dollar index and growing expectations of monetary easing by the Central Bank of Russia (CBR). CBR head Nabiullina mentioned that inflation had peaked and suggested that the second half of the year could see the start of a loosening cycle. The ruble-based MOEX Russia Index edged up to 3,456 on Friday, supported by the ongoing rally in industrial and precious metals, which boosted miners and metallurgists.


Turkey

The Turkish lira hit fresh record lows of 32.4 per USD, reflecting traders’ digestion of election results and concerns over the country’s economic outlook. President Erdogan’s AKP party experienced significant losses in local elections, including major cities like Istanbul and Ankara. This raised fears of a shift from recent orthodox economic policies to a more permissive fiscal stance. However, President Erdogan accepted the defeat and expressed confidence that his efforts to control inflation would yield results this year.
