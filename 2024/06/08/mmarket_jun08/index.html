Regional Markets - Fed Delay Sends Ripples

Summary

  • Both the Brazilian real and the Mexican peso, along with their respective stock markets, faltered this week due to the US Federal Reserve’s delayed decision and worsening economic conditions at home.

  • India, Russia, and Turkey all saw slight impacts from the delayed Federal Reserve decision, with nuances in domestic conditions such as India’s election outcomes, Russia’s capital controls bolstering its currency, and Turkey’s struggle with high inflation.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real weakened to 5.34 per USD as the US Federal Reserve delayed its interest rate cut, while a surge in government transfers heightened concerns over excessive deficits, leading to increased supply of BRL and inflation worries. Congress exacerbated this sentiment by approving measures allowing spending beyond the fiscal framework’s limits for the year. As a result, the Ibovespa plummeted by 1.7% to close at 120,767 on Friday.



Mexico

Similarly, the Mexican Peso depreciated beyond 18 per USD, marking its lowest level since October, driven by unexpected election outcomes and the Fed’s delay in decision-making. Traders rushed to unwind positions amidst concerns over potential reforms by the ruling Morena party, led by President-elect Claudia Sheinbaum, which could weaken checks on government power and increase interference in the economy. Mexico’s IPC index mirrored this decline, plunging nearly 3% to below 54,000, its lowest since December, reflecting a selloff in various Mexican asset classes following the country’s presidential elections.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.40% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, gave up 8.01% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee marginally weakened to 83.5 per USD, remaining relatively stable amidst mixed signals from the delayed US decision and domestic election outcomes. Despite this, Narendra Modi’s BJP secured a third term through a coalition government. On the economic front, the BSE Sensex surged approximately 3.69% higher on Friday, reaching a new record high and extending gains for the third consecutive session. The market rallied broadly due to anticipation of stability within the coalition government at the center and the RBI’s upward revision of its growth forecast for FY25 to 7.2%.

EU :

Russia

In June, the Russian ruble maintained stability around the 89 per USD mark for the intervention from Kremlin, hovering near 4-month highs. This was influenced by the Central Bank of Russia’s decision to hold its key rate at 16%, indicating a potential hike in the next meeting. Additionally, the ruble was supported by extensive capital controls implemented by the Kremlin, which artificially shielded the currency from pressure due to low foreign exchange inflows resulting from Western markets’ avoidance of Russian energy exports.


Turkey

The Turkish lira experienced slight depreciation this week, hovering around the 32.37 mark, largely due to the delay in the Federal Reserve’s decision. In May, the Turkish central bank maintained its interest rates at 50% and emphasized that monetary policy would be tightened if significant and persistent inflation deterioration is anticipated. The annual inflation rate climbed to 75.45% in May, leading the central bank to revise its year-end inflation forecast upward from 36% to 38%. Despite these challenges, the central bank remains optimistic that the tight monetary policy will gradually ease demand pressures by the second half of the year.
