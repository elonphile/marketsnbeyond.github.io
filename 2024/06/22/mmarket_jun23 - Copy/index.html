Regional Markets - Divergence in Latin America and Stability Elsewhere

Summary

  • Latin American markets diverged as Mexico’s currency strengthened on robust economic data post-elections, while Brazil faced pressure from a weakening real amid dollar strength and government restructuring efforts.

  • India maintained stability bolstered by robust economic fundamentals amidst external uncertainties, while Russia focused on policy responses to support its currency and Turkey showed signs of improvement with measures to boost market confidence.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real weakened to beyond 5.43 per USD in June, its lowest level in nearly two years due to a strong dollar outweighing earlier gains from the Brazilian Central Bank’s decision to hold its key rate steady at 10.5%. The real struggled amid ongoing selling pressure exacerbated by President Lula’s stance on public project expansion and conflicting signals from the planning and finance ministries. Despite this, the Ibovespa index climbed 0.7% to close at 121,341 on Friday, marking its fourth consecutive session of gains, driven by sustained market optimism post the central bank’s decision.



Mexico

The Mexican peso strengthened above 18.1 per USD, reaching a two-week high and rebounding from a fifteen-month low observed on June 12th. This recovery came as post-election selloff pressures eased, supported by strong economic data. April’s economic activity surged to 5.4% year-on-year, the sharpest increase since August 2022 and well above estimates of 3.8%. However, Mexican stock markets continued to experience volatility and selloffs across different asset classes following the presidential elections.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.28% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, gained 0.53% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee remained unchanged at 83.6 per USD, testing record lows in June amid broad-based weakness in Asian foreign exchange markets. The Russian ruble appreciated nearly 6% to 84 per USD, reaching a new 12-month high. This increase followed adjustments to last week’s US sanctions on key financial systems, which temporarily halted dollar and euro trading on the Moscow Exchange. The Central Bank of Russia maintained its key interest rate at 16% and hinted at a potential hike in its next meeting. The ruble’s strength was further supported by extensive capital controls implemented by the Kremlin to shield the currency from low foreign exchange inflows.The Sensex index also saw little change as markets awaited budget discussions under India’s new government and assessed the latest economic data for corporate outlook this year.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble appreciated to 89 per USD, reaching a new 12-month high. This increase followed adjustments to last week’s US sanctions on key financial systems, which temporarily halted dollar and euro trading on the Moscow Exchange. The Central Bank of Russia maintained its key interest rate at 16% and hinted at a potential hike in its next meeting. The ruble’s strength was further supported by extensive capital controls implemented by the Kremlin to shield the currency from low foreign exchange inflows.


Turkey

The Turkish lira hovered around 32.8 per USD, maintaining levels since late March’s local elections, as markets awaited Turkey’s economic restructuring efforts. The Turkish Central Bank kept interest rates unchanged at 50% in May. Despite economic challenges, the BIST 100 index reached 10,740 points, bolstered by increased domestic and foreign demand for Turkish lira-denominated financial assets. S&P Global upgraded Turkey’s credit rating from “B” to “B+,” citing improvements in monetary, fiscal, and income policy coordination as the central bank implemented measures to rebuild market confidence.
