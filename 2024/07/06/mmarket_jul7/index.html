Regional Markets - Fed Boosts Latin America, India Stable, Russia's Ruble Weakens, Turkey Volatile

Summary

  • Latin American currencies benefited from expectations surrounding the Federal Reserve’s upcoming monetary policy decisions. The prospect of potential interest rate cuts by the Fed boosted investor sentiment towards emerging market currencies in the region.

  • India maintained stability bolstered by robust economic fundamentals amidst external uncertainties, Russia’s ruble depreciated, influenced by reduced currency sales by exporters after fulfilling tax obligations. Meanwhile, Turkey’s lira strengthened on economic optimism but faced volatility from domestic policy announcements affecting investor sentiment.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real strengthened past 5.5 per USD, recovering from its over two-and-a-half-year low of 5.67 touched on July 2nd. This recovery was driven by a weakening greenback and extensive three-day meetings between President Lula da Silva and multiple ministers, which alleviated long-standing concerns over fiscal imbalances and unsustainable deficits. The Ibovespa edged 1.91% higher to close at 126,267 on Friday, marking a week of consistent gains amid easing concerns over fiscal balance and public spending.



Mexico

The Mexican peso strengthened towards 18.1 per USD, rebounding from a fifteen-month low of 18.75 seen on June 12th. This was driven by speculation about potential interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve, boosting demand for emerging market currencies. Banxico’s Deputy Governor Jonathan Heath echoed a cautious stance similar to Powell’s, emphasizing the importance of stable inflation data before considering rate adjustments, aligning with Mexico’s economic strategy.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.44% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, gained 1.10% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee traded near 83.5 per USD despite the weakening dollar, remaining robust. Foreign currency reserves held by the RBI rose to a record high in June, reflecting the robust growth outlook for India and bullish expectations for foreign investment. This allowed the central bank to maintain a conservative stance on currency intervention. The index posted a 1.2% weekly gain, its longest winning streak this year.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble traded around 88 per USD, impacted by reduced currency sales by exporters after fulfilling tax obligations. Despite recent US sanctions disrupting dollar and euro trading on the Moscow Exchange, the ruble maintained historically high levels. The Central Bank of Russia held its key interest rate at 16%, with indications of potential tightening in upcoming meetings. Russia’s MOEX stock index hovered near 3150 in early July, remaining subdued due to stringent capital controls and a cautious approach from the central bank amid fluctuating oil prices.


Turkey

Strengthening since June 26th, the Turkish lira traded around 32.7 per USD, bolstered by favorable local economic expectations and a weaker US dollar. International investor confidence slowly returned to Turkey as the country adopted more conventional economic policies. Despite positive economic indicators, the BIST 100 index declined to 10,852 points following announcements by Turkish Treasury and Finance Minister Mehmet Simsek regarding potential taxes on stock market gains.
