Regional Markets - Currencies Struggle Amid Stronger US Dollar and Domestic Economic Concerns

Summary

  • The Latin American markets were pressured this week by a stronger US dollar and domestic economic concerns, with the Brazilian real nearing a two-year low amid doubts over fiscal discipline and the Mexican peso weakening due to pessimistic IMF forecasts and economic slowdown.

  • India’s rupee weakened near record lows amid broad currency weakness and RBI constraints, while Russia’s ruble remained high despite reduced currency sales and US sanctions, and Turkey’s lira fell due to a stronger dollar


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real fell past 5.56 per USD driven by renewed concerns about fiscal discipline following recent comments by President Lula and a stronger US dollar. Investors became more cautious due to doubts over Brazil’s fiscal policy, sparked by Lula’s remarks questioning the prioritization of fiscal framework compliance amidst other national priorities. This uncertainty raised concerns about the government’s commitment to fiscal prudence. The Ibovespa ended the week with a loss of 0.99%, with Magazine Luiza down 0.6%, Lojas Renner declining 0.5%, and Localiza falling 0.4%.



Mexico

The Mexican peso weakened to 18.04 per USD, influenced by pessimistic IMF forecasts and a stronger US dollar. The IMF revised Mexico’s 2024 GDP growth outlook from 2.4% to 2.2%, citing an economic slowdown, particularly in manufacturing due to reduced US economic activity. This slowdown pressured Banxico to adopt a more accommodative stance. The main Mexican stock market index (IPC) closed the week with a 2.32% loss.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.58% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, gave up 8.54% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee depreciated to around 83.73 per USD, approaching its record low. Broad weakness in Asian currencies has limited the RBI’s ability to support the rupee. The usual defense measures against dollar buying could make Indian goods and services less competitive compared to Asian rivals in export markets. This challenge is highlighted by the RBI’s foreign currency reserves reaching a record high in July. The Sensex index is set to rise by 0.7% for the week, marking the seventh consecutive weekly gain, buoyed by expectations of Federal Reserve rate cuts and benefiting from the US-China trade tensions.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble hovered around 87.70 per USD, pressured by reduced currency sales by exporters following their ruble tax payments. Despite this, the ruble remains at historically high levels due to recent US sanctions on key financial systems, which have disrupted dollar and euro trading on the Moscow Exchange. The Central Bank of Russia previously maintained its key interest rate at 16% and signaled a possible hike at its next meeting.


Turkey

The Turkish lira depreciated to 33.15 per USD, impacted by a stronger dollar. However, international investors are gradually returning to the Turkish market and increasing their exposure. The adoption of more conventional policies seems to be paying off, with inflation falling for the first time in eight months to a lower-than-expected 71.6% in June.
