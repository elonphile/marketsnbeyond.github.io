Markets Recap - Bonds Rise, Stocks Subdued, Currencies Diverge

Summary

  • Despite positive momentum in most major indexes, subdued market activity prevailed as investors awaited developments, with US Treasuries yielding positive returns amid cautious anticipation of further inflation data and Federal Reserve signals. Similar as US, EU stock markets subdued, with slight gains during light trading ahead of the Easter holiday, with the STOXX Europe 600 Index reaching a record high, while bond yields declined on ECB’s rate cut hints.

  • The Asian markets witnessed declines as Japan’s stock market faced pressure amidst concerns over the yen’s depreciation, while Chinese equities dipped due to apprehensions surrounding the property sector downturn despite government pledges to support growth.

  • The currency market witnessed the dollar index retreating while the Japanese yen stabilized, WTI crude futures surged fueled by ongoing supply restrictions and geopolitical tensions, and gold maintained its high levels amid expectations of central bank rate cuts and geopolitical uncertainties.


US Market :

Despite a generally positive trajectory for most major indexes during the abbreviated trading week, market activity remained subdued as investors awaited further developments. The week was characterized by light news flow, with the exception of a significant event—the collapse of the Francis Scott Key Bridge in Baltimore on Tuesday. The collapse disrupted shipping access to the Port of Baltimore, prompting concerns about its economic impact. President Joe Biden assured federal assistance to reopen the port, though broader implications lingered uncertain.


US Treasuries delivered positive returns for the week, reflecting investor caution and a wait-and-see approach amid ongoing uncertainty. Investors remained attentive to forthcoming inflation data and awaited signals from the Federal Reserve regarding monetary policy.


Economic Data

  • US durable goods orders recorded a 1.4% increase in February, slightly surpassing expectations, indicating resilience in certain sectors.

  • New home sales unexpectedly declined in February, contrasting with prior reports of surging sales in existing homes, underscoring potential fluctuations in the housing market.

EU Market :

European markets experienced overall gains during a week characterized by light trading activity preceding the Easter holiday weekend. The STOXX Europe 600 Index notably reached a record intraday high, reflecting positive sentiment among investors.


European government bond yields observed a decline as investors reacted to signals from the European Central Bank (ECB). The ECB hinted at the possibility of a rate cut in June, contingent upon the trajectory of wage growth. Additionally, ECB council member Fabio Panetta underscored the potential for a shift in the rate cycle amid stagnant eurozone bank lending in February.


Economic Data

  • France inflation rate drops to 2.3% in March

  • Germany’s unemployment rate unchanged at 5.9% in March

  • UK’s GDP down 0.3% in Q4, confirms contraction

  • Germany’s retail sales drops 2.7% Y/Y in February

  • Spain’s GDP expands 2% in Q4 over previous year

  • Germany’s consumer confidence moves higher in April

  • Spain producer prices decreased sharply to 8.20% in February

Asian Market :

Japan’s stock markets faced declines through Thursday’s trading sessions, with investor attention primarily drawn to the yen’s significant depreciation, nearing JPY 152 against the U.S. dollar. Concerns heightened as the currency approached a perceived intervention threshold, prompting discussions among monetary authorities about potential market interventions to support the yen. Despite the yen’s weakness, large-cap exporters benefited from improved earnings due to increased overseas revenue.


Chinese stocks experienced a decline in the week leading up to Thursday, attributed to apprehensions surrounding the ongoing downturn in the property sector, which dampened investor sentiment. The Shanghai Composite Index and the blue chip CSI 300 both retreated, reflecting investor caution. Despite this, Chinese Premier Li Qiang expressed openness to foreign investment at the China Development Forum, promising government support for growth across various sectors, including biological manufacturing, artificial intelligence, and the data economy. However, investors remained vigilant for further policy support from the Chinese government to bolster economic growth.


Economic Data :

  • Japan’s Tokyo inflation slows as expected in March

  • Japan’s industrial output slips 0.1% in February, retail sales exceed forecasts

  • China Industrial Profits swing to growth in Jan-Feb

Other Markets :

The dollar index retreated to below 104.5 on Friday after hitting a six-week high of 104.7, with investors processing a slew of economic data during the low-volume Good Friday session. Both headline and core PCE deflators, crucial indicators of inflation for the US economy, failed to surprise on the upside. The Japanese yen stabilized near 151 per dollar, rebounding slightly from recent 34-year lows, as Japanese authorities indicated their readiness to intervene in currency markets.


WTI crude futures surged 3.09% to settle at $83.12 per barrel on Friday, marking a third consecutive monthly gain. The rally was fueled by ongoing efforts by the OPEC+ alliance to restrict supply and persistent geopolitical risks in Eastern Europe and the Middle East. Following OPEC+’s decision to extend voluntary production cuts through the second quarter, analysts anticipate the alliance to maintain current output policies in their upcoming meeting. Recent drone attacks on Russian refineries in Ukraine and continued disruptions in supply routes from Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping added to the bullish sentiment in oil markets.


Gold maintained levels above $2,232 an ounce in thin holiday trading on Friday, hovering near all-time highs amid expectations of interest rate cuts by major central banks and heightened geopolitical tensions. The metal is poised to record a more than 9% gain for March. Despite hot inflation data, the US Federal Reserve remained steadfast in its outlook for three rate cuts this year during its latest policy meeting, with markets anticipating the commencement of rate reductions in June.
