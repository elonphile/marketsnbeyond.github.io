Regional Markets - Currency Reaction On National Bank's Decision

Summary

  • In Latin America, both the Brazilian real and The Mexican pesoweakened as investors assessed interest rate decisions in Brazil and the US, with the Brazilian and Mexican central bank cutting rates while the US Fed hinted at potential cuts.

  • The Indian rupee hit a record low against the USD amidst broad currency sell-offs, while robust economic growth in India supported sentiment.

  • The Russian ruble outperformed amid the CBR’s decision to maintain interest rates and strong oil prices, while the Turkish lira saw slight appreciation following the central bank’s unexpected rate hike amidst high inflation and fiscal concerns.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real stabilized around 4.99 per USD this week as investors weighed interest rate decisions in Brazil and the United States. The Brazilian central bank reduced the Selic rate by another 50 bps to 10.75%, emphasizing the importance of fiscal targets for stabilizing inflation expectations. Meanwhile, the US Fed maintained its interest rates as expected but hinted at potential rate cuts this year, which influenced the performance of the greenback. Brazil’s industry ministry has launched a number of investigations into the alleged dumping of industrial products by China as Latin America’s largest economy reels from a wave of cheap imported goods.



Mexico

Similar to Brazil, the Mexican peso weakened toward 16.75 per USD, retracting from its recent high near the highest level since November 2015 of 16.66 USD recorded on March 13th. Investors processed Banxico’s rate decision, with the Bank of Mexico reducing its benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points to 11.00%, meeting expectations after seven consecutive pauses. Despite a temporary uptick in inflation, annual inflation decreased to 4.40% in February 2024, primarily due to reversals in non-core components, while core inflation continued its downward trend.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.47% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, surged 14.82% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee plunged to a record low of 83.58 per USD as China loosened the grip on its yuan fixing, triggering a broad selloff for Asian currencies. This was magnified by market observations that the RBI likely refrained from its regular extent of intervention to prevent aggressive depreciation. EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble appreciated towards 92 per USD amid a volatile session, as investors mulled over the regulator’s decision to maintain the key interest rate unchanged at 16%. Persistently stubborn inflation and strong consumer demand prompted the CBR to hold rates steady. The ruble was supported by elevated oil prices and nearing end-of-month tax period, contributing to its strength.


Turkey

The Turkish lira appreciated slightly to 32 per USD after the central bank unexpectedly raised interest rates by 500bps, pushing the one-week repo rate to 50%. High inflation rates in February and concerns about loose fiscal policy contributed to a challenging economic outlook. Turkish equities saw gains as markets assessed the economy’s outlook following the central bank’s unexpected resumption of its tightening cycle.
