Stocks Recap - EV Carmakers Mixed, Tech Suppliers Surge

Summary

  • EV carmakers had mixed performance. Asian companies saw slight gains, while European and American counterparts, including Tesla, faced losses. Japanese manufacturers benefited from positive news, but concerns over weak earnings affected sentiment towards NIO. Li Auto declined after launching a new model, and Tesla experienced production setbacks in Germany due to an arson attack.

  • TSM and ASML, prominent EV suppliers in this week’s portfolio, witnessed substantial gains, particularly TSM, whose Q4 data surpassed expectations with a revenue of $20.10 billion, signaling an optimistic 2024 outlook driven by recovering chip demand and strategic expansions.

  • Commodity markets experienced a surge in prices, while North American tech companies saw mixed performance with NVIDIA posting significant gains and Gitlab facing notable losses.


CARMAKER :


As of Wednesday’s closing price, the performance of EV carmakers for the week was mixed. Asian car companies experienced slight gains, led by Toyota with a 4.61% increase. However, European and American car companies faced losses, notably Tesla with a significant decline of -12.57%, alongside VinFast and BMW.


Weekly Winners:


Japanese car companies saw stock prices reach record highs, benefiting from the depreciation of the yen in recent months, which has favored export manufacturers. Notable winners include:

  • Toyota announced a $2.22 billion investment in Brazil.
  • Honda’s North American unit reported a 32% rise in sales in February.
  • BYD reduced the starting price of its cheapest car amidst intensifying EV competition, offering the lower-priced Yuan Plus crossover.

NIO’s performance remained mediocre due to strong sales but weak earnings, falling at the lower end of expectations for Q1.



Weekly Losers:


Li Auto experienced a sharp decline of over 10% this week, possibly due to the launch of its new mega model. While company officials express confidence in the model’s success, investors remain skeptical, particularly as the official has not disclosed the number of orders.


Tesla faced production setbacks this week, as an arson attack halted production in Germany. Production is expected to remain suspended until the end of next week due to extensive repairs needed, resulting in power outages for thousands of nearby houses. Additionally, Tesla removed price incentives for Model Y variants.

HIDDEN CHAMPION :


As of Wednesday’s closing price, EV carmakers’ suppliers witnessed a mixed performance for the week. Companies associated with automotive chips continued to perform well, with notable gains seen in:


  • TSM, which surged by 10%.
  • AML, showing a 4.79% increase.
  • Mobileye, exceeding expectations with a gain of 9.19%.

However, the performance of manufacturers in Japan and Germany was mediocre, with some small losses. The biggest loser was ChargePoint, experiencing a 7.25% decline.


Weekly Winners:


AI-related manufacturers, such as ASML and CATL, emerged as potentially the biggest winners of the AI arms race. This week, ASML achieved a significant milestone by reaching the ‘first light’ landmark on its new EUV lithography system.


Additionally, India’s approval of a $15.2 billion investment in chip manufacturing and Mobileye’s positive conference commentary led to a surge in shares, gaining over 10% intraday. Despite a recent share value decline, Mobileye’s EyeQ technology, capable of complex vision tasks, has been adopted by more than 50 vehicle manufacturers. From the lastest news, the vast majority of excess inventory would be cleared out, which includes a significant number of customer commitments they’ve received for the second quarter.


Weekly Loser:


ChargePoint reported a Q4 revenue 24% lower than the previous year, attributing the decline to slowing EV adoption impacting commercial sales. Quarterly revenue fell to $115.8 million from $152.8 million, resulting in a 4% intraday share price decline. German suppliers also saw mediocre performance, with Siemens Energy securing a new €4 billion credit line after facing losses with Gamesa.


Weekly Catalyst Highlights :


  • GM, Wipro, and Magna partner on automobile software platform

  • Dell’s AI strength buoys chip stocks sailing into weekend

  • Zuckerberg meets South Korean President; looks to Samsung to diversify risk

  • Intel’s new programmable chip unit could be boosted by AI

  • India approves $15.2B worth chip manufacturing investment

  • ASML gets to ‘first light’ landmark on new EUV lithography system

COMMODITY :


With the exception of Albemarle (ALB), other manufacturers performed well this week. Particularly noteworthy were CMOC, which saw gains of 6.99%, and Glencore, with gains of 3.73%.


Albemarle experienced a significant decline in its stock price due to concerns over its ability to fund lithium projects. The company’s shares plummeted after announcing plans to offer $1.75 billion of depositary shares, reflecting investor worries about the potential impact on its financial stability.
