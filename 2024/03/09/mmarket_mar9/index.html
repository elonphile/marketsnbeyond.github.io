Regional Markets - Emerging Markets React to Economic Strength and US Rate Cut Speculation

Summary

  • In the emerging markets, most currencies appreciated due to the robust economy and potential US rate cuts. the Brazilian real depreciated for the concerns on its interest rate hikes while the Mexican peso strengthened amid speculation about potential US interest rate cuts and subdued Mexican inflation figures.

  • The Indian rupee held firm, supported by expectations of US monetary policy easing and India’s robust economic performance, including a GDP growth rate exceeding market expectations. The Russian ruble strengthened on expectations of increased foreign currency inflow, while the Turkish lira faced record lows amidst concerns over high inflation and economic outlook.


Latin America :

Brazil

For Brazil, the currency depreciated this week despite the Federal Reserve’s less hawkish stance on interest rates, as investors remained concerned about ongoing interest rate hikes in the Brazilian economy. The Brazilian real traded around 4.95 per USD, approaching a one-week high of 4.93 per USD on February 27th. In the stock markets, the Ibovespa declined by 1.02%, closing at 127,036 on Friday, primarily due to significant losses from the state-owned oil giant Petrobras. Petrobras’ decision not to declare extraordinary dividends prompted downgrades from several major financial institutions, including Santander, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Safra, and Goldman Sachs. These downgrades cited increased uncertainties regarding capital allocation risk, leading to a 10.25% decline in Petrobras shares.



Mexico

The Mexican peso strengthened toward the 16.8 per USD mark in early March, reaching an over six-month high, driven by a weakening US dollar and positive economic data. The release of economic data heightened speculation about a potential interest rate cut by the US Federal Reserve in June, which weakened support for the greenback. Investors are eagerly awaiting the Bank of Mexico’s decision on March 21 following subdued Mexican inflation figures. In February, the year-on-year inflation rate was 4.40%, slightly below the estimated 4.42% and January’s 4.88%. The monthly Consumer Price Index decreased marginally from 0.11% in January to 0.09%. Although the Core CPI, excluding volatile items, exceeded expectations by rising to 4.64%, it was lower than the previous 4.76%. Additionally, the monthly core CPI aligned with estimates, increasing from 0.40% to 0.49% in February.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate slumped by 0.42% for the week. Notably, Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, gave up 4.99% this week as investors are concerned about the domestic economy.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee still remained strong, hovering 83 per USD, retracting from its four-month high of 82.2 on January 15th, as expectations of US monetory policy easing and robust economy for India. The country’s GDP expanded by 8.4% from the previous year in the December quarter, well above market expectations of 6.6%, to lift the second estimate of the current fiscal GDP to a 7.6% growth rate.

EU :

Russia

Russian currency and index benefit from the pocily stance from EU,US and rising oil price. The Russian ruble appreciated to around 90.5 per USD, marking its highest level in a month. This surge was fueled by expectations of increased foreign currency inflow from exporters’ tax payments, following reports of strong oil and gas revenues. According to the country’s finance ministry report, oil and gas revenues crucial for the economy are anticipated to rise to 132.1 billion rubles in March 2024. Additionally, a weaker global dollar index and larger sales of foreign currency during the dividend season are expected to provide further support. Markets are now eagerly awaiting a decision on the possible extension of capital controls for the largest firms from April until the end of the year, while also closely monitoring the inflation outlook for cues about the start of monetary easing.


Turkey

Turkey’s lira underperformed among emerging markets due to concerns about inflation and the economy. The Turkish lira extended its losses, reaching fresh record lows past 31 per USD, as traders continued to assess Turkey’s monetary and economic outlook. Annual inflation accelerated for a fourth consecutive month to 67% in February, the highest level since November 2022. The finance minister anticipates that inflation will remain elevated due to base effects and the delayed impact of rate hikes, but projects a decline within the next year. Concerns persist about a stronger depreciation of the lira after the local elections on March 31st, similar to the significant fall seen in May last year following the presidential and general elections.
