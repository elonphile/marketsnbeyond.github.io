March 2, 2024

Regional Markets - Gains Across the Board, Except for Turkey

Summary

  • In the emerging markets, most currencies appreciated due to the robust economy and potential US rate cuts based on the inflation news except Turkey. Latin American currencies and stocks navigated mixed pressures, with the Brazilian real buoyed by positive economic indicators while the Mexican peso strengthened amid signs of resilience, albeit facing challenges in the equity market.

  • The Indian rupee held firm, supported by expectations of US monetary policy easing and India’s robust economic performance, including a GDP growth rate exceeding market expectations. The Russian ruble stabilized amid diminishing sanctions fears and rising oil prices, while Turkey’s lira struggled due to concerns over inflation and economic stability.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real hovered around 4.95 per USD, near a one-week high, buoyed by positive GDP figures and a robust manufacturing PMI, which reduced pressure for further Selic rate cuts amid US potential interest rate adjustments. Minister of Finance Fernando Haddad celebrated Brazil’s 2.9% economic growth in 2023, surpassing initial expectations. S&P Global Manufacturing PMI rose to 54.1, indicating sustained factory expansion, driven by improved domestic demand. However, the Ibovespa ended slightly lower, with a 0.18% loss.



Mexico

The Mexican peso strengthened towards the 17 per USD level, reaching a six-week high, supported by signs of economic resilience and expectations of US monetary easing. S&P Global Manufacturing PMI indicated robust business activity at 52.3, potentially delaying Bank of Mexico’s monetary policy easing in March. However, the main stock market index in Mexico (IPC Mexico) declined by 3.22% since the beginning of 2024.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate slumped by 0.49% for the week. Notably, Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, surged 4.58% this week as investors are concerned about the domestic economy.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee still remained strong, hovering 83 per USD, retracting from its four-month high of 82.2 on January 15th, as expectations of US monetory policy easing and robust economy for India. The country’s GDP expanded by 8.4% from the previous year in the December quarter, well above market expectations of 6.6%, to lift the second estimate of the current fiscal GDP to a 7.6% growth rate.

EU :

Russia

Russian currency and index benefit from the pocily stance from EU,US and rising oil price. The Russian ruble stabilized around 91.5 per USD, reaching its highest level in over two weeks, as concerns regarding US and European sanctions diminished. Additionally, rising oil prices boosted foreign currency inflows, supporting the ruble alongside tight monetary conditions and increased government spending. The ruble-based MOEX Russia index saw mixed dynamics, closing at 3,266 on Friday, with the IT sector rebounding while construction and transport sectors experienced declines. Individual stocks like Yandex, NLMK, Severstal, Rostelecom, Lukoil, and VTB showed varied performance.


Turkey

Turkey’s lira underperformed among emerging markets due to concerns about inflation and the economy. Despite this, Turkey’s main stock market index, BIST 100, continued to reach record highs, approaching the 9400 level in February, with year-to-date gains of about 25%. The Central Bank of Turkey maintained interest rates during its February 2024 meeting, aligning with market expectations. However, under new leadership, the bank adopted a more hawkish stance, hinting at potential policy tightening in response to anticipated inflationary pressures. Turkey’s inflation rate exceeded expectations in January, reaching 64.86%, with Governor Fatih Karahan attributing the increase to the impact of the minimum wage hike on the consumer price index (CPI).
