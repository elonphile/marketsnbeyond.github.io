Markets Recap - Divergent Paths Amid Inflationary Pressures and Monetary Policy Speculations

Summary

  • The US and EU markets exhibited divergent reactions this week, largely influenced by contrasting performances in inflation. While US stocks surged following favorable inflation data, prompting speculation of imminent interest rate cuts, EU markets saw mixed results as sticky inflation figures led investors to reassess expectations for rate cuts by the European Central Bank.

  • Asian stocks surged, particularly in Japan, buoyed by a weak yen and record highs for the Nikkei 225, while in China, hopes for increased monetary easing offset concerns over manufacturing declines.

  • Currency markets saw the dollar weaken while the euro and yen strengthened ahead of anticipated shifts in monetary policy, while oil prices surged amid supply concerns and geopolitical tensions, propelling gold to record highs amid a weaker dollar and softer economic data.


US Market :

Stocks mostly ended the week higher, with the Nasdaq Composite and S&P 500 Index both reaching record highs for the first time in over two years, buoyed by favorable inflation news. Despite the positive sentiment, the Federal Reserve’s communication indicated no immediate plans for an interest rate cut, tempering market expectations.


Bond markets reflected positively in respnse to the PCE data. The release of the Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index data contributed to the 10-year Treasury yield hitting its lowest intraday level since February 13, suggesting investors are anticipating a potential rate cut in the near future.


Economic Data

  • The core PCE price index rising 2.8% for the 12 months ended in January, in line with expectations.

  • The Institute for Supply Management’s gauge of manufacturing activity fell below expectations, dropping from an 18-month high in January to 47.8 in February.

EU Market :

Most major indexes ended the week slightly higher, nearing record highs, except for the UK’s FTSE 100 Index, which experienced a slight decline. Germany’s DAX and Italy’s FTSE MIB saw gains of 1.81% and 0.68%, respectively, while France’s CAC 40 Index lost 0.41%. European government bond yields rose across the board.


In the bond market, sticky inflation data prompted investors to reconsider expectations for interest rate cuts by the European Central Bank in 2024, as both headline and core inflation slowed less than anticipated in February. Annual consumer price growth in the eurozone marginally slowed to 2.6%, while core inflation remained elevated at 3.1%.


Economic Data

  • European Commission’s economic sentiment indicator unexpectedly declined to 95.4 in February. Although industrial sector confidence remained stable, the services sector saw a decline due to lower demand.

  • In Germany, consumer price growth continued to decelerate, while French manufacturing activity contracted slightly less than initially reported in February.

  • In UK, the Nationwide Building Society reported a sequential increase in house prices in February, marking the first annualized increase since January 2023.

Asian Market :

Japanese stocks continued their upward trend, driven by the historical weakness of the yen, particularly benefiting Japan’s exporters. The Nikkei 225 gained around 2.1% during the week, hovering near record highs and marking February’s gains at about 10.0%. In the bond market, yields saw a slight decline as the Bank of Japan signaled a cautious approach, emphasizing the need for sustained inflation alongside wage growth before any policy shifts. Consumer inflation moderated in January, with the core consumer price index slowing to 2.0% year-on-year.


In China, hopes for increased monetary easing measures to stimulate growth supported the stock market, especially amid a backdrop of worsening manufacturing activity. Chinese banks approved over RMB 200 billion in development loans to fund unfinished residential projects, boosting liquidity in the real estate market. The Shanghai Composite Index gained 0.74%, while the CSI 300 added 1.38%. However, the Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong retreated by 0.82%.


Economic Data :

  • China saw a deterioration in manufacturing conditions in February, with their respective purchasing managers’ indices (PMIs) falling below the 50-mark threshold indicating contraction.

  • Japan’s manufacturing PMI declined to 49.1, driven by decreases in production and exports, while China’s official manufacturing PMI also fell to 49.1.

  • China’s non-manufacturing PMI surpassed expectations, rising to 51.4 from 50.7 in January.

Other Markets :

In the currency market, the dollar depreciated while the euro and yen appreciated amid anticipation of upcoming monetary policy changes. Investors expect the US to lead interest rate cuts, followed by the ECB, driven by recent inflation data. The euro fluctuated around $1.08 as traders assessed inflation figures and monetary policy outlook. The dollar index fell below 104 as investors reacted to disappointing US economic data and comments from central bank officials.


WTI crude futures surged to around $80 per barrel, reaching a four-month high, driven by speculation of OPEC+ extending supply cuts and ongoing geopolitical tensions. Concerns about ceasefire talks between Israel and Hamas, as well as Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping, added a risk premium to oil prices. Additionally, escalating tensions in Ukraine, with warnings of a wider conflict from Putin, further heightened pressure on oil markets.


Gold climbed to over $2,080 per ounce, hitting an all-time high and recording its second consecutive weekly gain, fueled by a weakening dollar and lower Treasury yields, supported by softening US economic data. Weakness in US manufacturing and consumer sentiment indicators contributed to gold’s rally.
