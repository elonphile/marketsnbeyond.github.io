Markets Recap - US Downturn, EU Positivity, and Mixed Performance in Asia Amid Central Bank Signals and Economic Uncertainties

Summary

  • The US markets witnessed a downturn this week as surprising inflation data and signs of consumer spending moderation led to varied sector performances, with energy stocks outperforming while technology shares lagged, compounded by concerns over the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance on interest rate cuts.

  • Despite concerns over sticky US inflation data, EU stock markets ended the week positively driven by strong corporate earnings, while slight upticks in bond yields reflected lingering uncertainty around central bank interest rate cuts. Asian markets saw mixed performance as Japan’s stocks declined amid concerns over the BoJ’s policy stance, while Chinese equities rose on government stabilization measures, reflecting divergent economic outlooks.

  • Amid central bank signals and economic uncertainties, currency markets saw appreciation for dollar this week, while oil prices surged on geopolitical tensions, and gold held steady near all-time highs, acting as a hedge against inflation and geopolitical risks.


US Market :

The US stock markets experienced a downturn this week as investors grappled with surprising inflation data and signs of consumer spending moderation. While the Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a record high midweek, it retreated by week’s end. Energy sector stocks notably outperformed due to elevated oil prices, contrasting with technology shares, which lagged primarily due to underperformance in NVIDIA and other chipmakers. Additionally, a key Apple supplier saw a significant decline in share value, reflecting market concerns about a slowdown in 5G and renewable energy markets.


The bond markets reacted notably to the inflation data, triggering concerns about the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance. This stance indicates reduced possibilities for interest rate cuts in the near term, impacting market sentiment. The yield on the benchmark 10-year Treasury note surged to its highest intraday level since February 27, reaching 4.32%.


Economic Data

  • The latest economic data revealed a 0.4% rise in the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for February, aligning with consensus expectations.

  • Core prices, excluding food and energy, saw a slightly higher increase of 0.4%. Meanwhile, the Producer Price Index (PPI) surged by 0.6% in February, double consensus estimates, and marking the highest increase in six months.

  • Year-over-year, headline producer prices rose to 1.6%, surpassing expectations. Retail sales grew by 0.6% in February, albeit missing expectations, with increased gasoline prices contributing significantly.

  • The University of Michigan’s Consumer Sentiment survey hinted at a modest decline in consumer expectations, adding to the week’s mixed economic signals.

EU Market :

The EU stock markets ended the week on a positive note, driven by encouraging corporate earnings offsetting concerns stemming from sticky US inflation data. France’s CAC 40 index notably outperformed, propelled by strong gains in luxury sector heavyweights such as Hermès. London’s energy-centric FTSE 100 also saw gains, buoyed by the performance of energy groups amid rising oil prices. The pan-European STOXX Europe 600 index extended its winning streak to eight weeks, adding 0.31% for the week. France’s CAC 40 index rose by 1.70%, Italy’s FTSE MIB gained 1.84%, and Germany’s DAX added 0.69%, while the UK’s FTSE 100 index edged up by 0.31%.


The bond markets experienced a slight uptick in the 10-year government yield, influenced by persistent inflation readings from the US that eroded confidence in potential interest rate cuts by central banks. Despite this, ECB officials reiterated their commitment to addressing deflationary pressures. In an interview on Wednesday, Latvian central banker Martins Kazaks suggested imminent rate cuts, expressing confidence that inflationary pressures were being effectively managed. Other ECB policymakers, including Francois Villeroy de Galhau, Robert Holzmann, and Pierre Wunsch, echoed similar sentiments, advocating for a possible interest rate cut by June.


Economic Data

  • France inflation drops to 3% in February

  • ECB Governing Council member Stournaras pushes for two rate cuts before August

  • Spain’s inflation drops to 2.8% in February, lowest in six months

  • UK’s GDP surges 0.20% in January as expected

  • UK’s unemployment rate rises to 3.9% in January, while wage growth, excluding bonuses, fell to 6.1%, the lowest level since mid-2022

  • Germany’s inflation rate drops to 2.5% in February

Asian Market :

Japan’s stock markets saw a downturn this week, with the Nikkei 225 Index recording a loss of 2.5%. Concurrently, the bond markets witnessed a rise in yields following announcements regarding wage growth. The potential for the Bank of Japan (BoJ) to end its negative interest rate policy heightened with news of the highest average wage increases for Japan’s labor unions since the early 1990s. BoJ Governor Kazuo Ueda maintained a cautious outlook on the country’s prospects, noting moderate economic recovery alongside weaknesses in certain data points.


Chinese equities experienced gains as recent government interventions to stabilize the market bolstered investor confidence, despite a less optimistic economic outlook. The Shanghai Composite Index rose by 0.28%, while the blue-chip CSI 300 added 0.71% for the week. The People’s Bank of China injected RMB 387 billion into the banking system via its medium-term lending facility, keeping the lending rate unchanged at 2.5%. This move, alongside the expiration of RMB 481 billion in loans this month, resulted in a net withdrawal of RMB 94 billion from the banking system, marking the first such cash extraction through this liquidity instrument since November 2022.


Economic Data :

  • China’s new home prices drop by 1.4% Y/Y in February

  • Japan’s economy avoids recession as strong capex boosts Q4 GDP

  • China’s consumer prices rise for first time in six months

  • Japan GDP in the fourth quarter of 2023 expanded 0.1% on the quarter compared with the earlier release suggesting the economy had contracted 0.1%. On an annualized basis, this equated to a 0.4% expansion versus a prior fall of 0.4%.

Other Markets :

The dollar strengthened this week amidst expectations of interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve in June, while the Bank of Japan (BoJ) signaled a potential end to its negative interest rate policy. The yen dipped below 148.5 against the USD, retreating from recent five-week highs as investor sentiment shifted.


Oil prices surged this week driven by geopolitical tensions stemming from attacks in Ukraine and reports from the International Energy Agency (IEA). Ukrainian drone attacks on Russian refineries heightened concerns, particularly with a fire breaking out at Rosneft’s largest refinery. Additionally, the IEA reported an unexpected decline in US crude inventories, along with revised upward forecasts for global oil demand, contributing to bullish sentiment in the oil market.


Gold held steady near $2,160 an ounce on Friday, marking its first weekly decline in four amidst uncertainty surrounding potential Fed rate cuts. Hotter-than-anticipated CPI and PPI data, coupled with lower initial jobless claims, prompted investors to reconsider expectations for monetary easing. However, gold remained close to its all-time highs, serving as a hedge against inflation and geopolitical risks following Russia’s movement of tactical nuclear weapons closer to Nato.
