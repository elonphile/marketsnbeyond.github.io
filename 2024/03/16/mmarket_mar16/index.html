Regional Markets - Brazilian Real and Turkish Lira Struggle, Indian Rupee Holds Strong Amidst Global Headwinds

Summary

  • In the emerging markets, most currencies deppreciated due to delay for potential US rate cuts from sticky inflation data, except the Mexican peso. Latin markets saw mixed performance this week, with the Brazilian real depreciating amid volatility in the stock market, the Mexican peso weakening amidst positive US economic indicators, and the Argentine stock index rising on government debt rollover efforts and favorable inflation data.

  • Despite a temporary weakening due to US inflation data, the Indian rupee remained strong, buoyed by robust economic growth, subdued inflation, and supportive measures from the Reserve Bank of India. The Russian ruble stabilized amid expectations of increased foreign currency inflows, while the Turkish lira continued its descent to record lows amidst concerns over persistently high inflation and looming local elections.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real depreciated against the dollar this week, driven by the dollar’s strength and negative news affecting the stock market. Brazilian stocks experienced volatility as investors reacted to the decision by state-owned oil giant Petroleo Brasileiro not to pay extraordinary dividends. This move sparked tension between shareholders and the government, as funds retained by Petrobras could be allocated to fiscal purposes.



Mexico

In contrast to other currencies, the Mexican peso weakened below 16.7 per USD this week, stepping back from its highest level since November 2015, driven by positive economic data. This decline came amidst positive economic indicators from the US, including a rebound in retail sales and rising producer-side inflation, which bolstered the US dollar and diminished expectations for Fed rate cuts. Investors are eagerly awaiting the Bank of Mexico’s decision on March 21 following subdued Mexican inflation figures. In February, the year-on-year inflation rate was 4.40%, slightly below the estimated 4.42% and January’s 4.88%. The monthly Consumer Price Index decreased marginally from 0.11% in January to 0.09%. Although the Core CPI, excluding volatile items, exceeded expectations by rising to 4.64%, it was lower than the previous 4.76%. Additionally, the monthly core CPI aligned with estimates, increasing from 0.40% to 0.49% in February.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.47% for the week. Notably, The Argentine stock index ended higher this week, supported by a debt rollover initiative by the government. Argentina exchanged over USD 50 billion in outstanding peso-denominated debt maturing this year for new, longer-maturity bonds, covering approximately 77% of the country’s peso-denominated notes due in 2024. Additionally, Argentina received positive news on the inflation front, with consumer prices increasing by 13.2% in February, notably below the consensus estimate of 15%.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee weakened to 82.8 per USD this week, influenced by a larger-than-expected inflation print in the US, which bolstered the USD slightly. However, despite this setback, the rupee remains robust, reaching levels not seen since late August 2023, supported by significant inflows. Foreign interest in Indian government bonds is increasing, driven by anticipation of potential inclusion of local bonds in the FTSE Russell’s EM bond gauge.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble stabilized around 91.5 per USD, supported by expectations of increased foreign currency inflows from exporters’ tax payments amid higher oil prices and the dividends season. Forecasts from the country’s finance ministry anticipate a climb in oil and gas revenues to 132.1 billion rubles in March 2024. Additionally, capital controls for exporters and tight monetary conditions contributed to the ruble’s stability. The ruble-based MOEX Russia index closed slightly muted at 3,300 on Friday, following brief declines over two sessions. Investors remained cautious amidst ongoing presidential elections, which are scheduled to continue until March 17th.


Turkey

The Turkish lira extended its losses, reaching fresh record lows surpassing 31 per USD, as market participants continued to evaluate Turkey’s monetary and economic prospects. Annual inflation surged for the fourth consecutive month to 67% in February, marking the highest level since November 2022. The finance minister expects inflation to remain elevated due to base effects and the delayed impact of rate hikes but anticipates a decline within the next year. Concerns persist regarding the potential for further lira depreciation following the local elections scheduled for March 31st, reminiscent of the significant decline observed last year after the presidential and general elections in May.
