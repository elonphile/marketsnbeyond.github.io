Stocks Recap - Workhorse Stumbles, Rivian and Chinese Automakers Rise; CATL Soars Amidst EV Boom

Summary

  • EV carmakers had mixed performance. Despite Workhorse’s substantial losses following a disappointing Q4 earnings report, Rivian and various Chinese carmakers, including BYD and Li Auto, surged in the market this week, driven by strategic initiatives, positive investor sentiment, and impressive sales figures, showcasing the dynamic nature of the automotive sector.

  • CATL surged following an upgraded rating, benefitting from the electric vehicle market growth, while Japanese suppliers faced declines possibly due to yen strengthening.

  • This week witnessed contrasting fortunes in the market, with Vale and BHP facing notable losses while Glencore soared on Deutsche Bank’s upgrade, while RIO and Petrobras struggled due to weak fundamentals and dividend frustrations, respectively.


CARMAKER :


This week’s market analysis reveals notable shifts, particularly evident from Tuesday’s closing prices. The standout performers were Rivian and several Chinese car manufacturers, with Rivian leading the pack with a remarkable 12.15% weekly gain, closely followed by BYD at 8.76%. Conversely, Workhorse emerged as the week’s biggest loser, experiencing a significant downturn of -9.10%, trailed closely by Toyota with a loss of -8.54%.


Weekly Winners:


Rivian’s impressive performance stems from its unveiling of new models and plans to expand production capacity at its Illinois plant.


Meanwhile, Chinese car manufacturers, notably BYD, showcased remarkable resilience, with BYD boasting an 8.76% weekly gain. BYD’s upward trajectory is likely fueled by its recent performance and positive investor sentiment.


Li Auto also witnessed a surge, with Tuesday’s intraday gain hitting 8.91%. Li Auto’s surge can be attributed to the release of its Q4 annual reports, surpassing market expectations, particularly in new energy vehicle sales, which soared by 136.4% year-on-year.



Weekly Losers:


Workhorse faced substantial losses, plummeting by 9.10% for the week. The downturn came in the wake of the company’s disappointing Q4 earnings report, despite efforts to implement cost-cutting measures and withholding guidance.


Honda witnessed losses, primarily attributed to vehicles under NHTSA review for braking issues.


Weekly Catalyst Highlights :


  • General Motors taps former Tesla exec to head global manufacturing unit

  • Tesla, Ford, GM among automakers to get low ratings for their partial automation systems

  • Porsche sees lower return on sales in 2024, targets launch of four new models

  • Ford to pay $365M to settle customs civil penalty claims

  • Honda vehicles under NHTSA review for braking issue

  • GM resumes sale of Chevy Blazer EV with discounts

HIDDEN CHAMPION :


Among the standout performers, China’s CATL, a prominent lithium battery manufacturer, takes the spotlight with an impressive 12.87% gain. However, the majority of other performances hover around a modest 2% mark, with TSM maintaining its steady uptrend. Conversely, several companies across Europe, America, and Japan faced downturns this week, led by Continental with a significant 7.20% loss, followed closely by Panasonic with a 6.20% decline. ASML, after several weeks of steady growth, experienced a 2.18% decline.



Weekly Winners:


CATL’s remarkable surge was catalyzed by Morgan Stanley’s upgraded rating, fueled by the company’s advantageous position within the rapidly expanding electric vehicle market and the surging demand for batteries.


TSM, despite potential concerns stemming from policy impacts on its downstream customer Intel, continues to exhibit resilience, buoyed by robust demand and promising prospects, including potential federal grants for its Arizona chip production.



Weekly Loser:


Continental’s poor performance can be attributed to the unveiling of its annual figures and the introduction of self-imposed targets for the current year, leading to a dip in share prices to levels not seen since December. Despite reporting a 31.6% rise in adjusted earnings before interest and tax, reaching €2.5bn, the company acknowledged facing challenges in 2023. Additionally, news of the company’s Chief Financial Officer, Katja Vila, opting not to renew her contract further exacerbated investor concerns.


Japanese companies, including Panasonic and Nidec, witnessed declines, potentially influenced by the recent strengthening of the yen, prompting reactions in the Japanese stock market.


Weekly Catalyst Highlights :


  • Pentagon said to end plan for $2.5B Intel grant

COMMODITY :


This week’s market analysis highlights notable winners and losers, with Vale experiencing the most significant setback, recording a 9.43% loss, closely trailed by BHP. Conversely, CMOC and ALB showcased robust performances, alongside Glencore, which particularly stood out after receiving an upgrade from Deutsche Bank, recognizing its leadership within the sector, resulting in a surge in its stock price.


RIO faced challenges this week, primarily attributed to a decline in iron ore prices, reaching fresh four-month lows due to persistently weak China fundamentals. This downturn impacted investor sentiment, reflecting negatively on RIO’s stock performance.


Petrobras encountered substantial losses following a wave of analyst downgrades amid frustrations over dividend policies. The company’s dividend performance this week contributed to the decline in its value, exacerbated by additional concerns surrounding political issues related to the CEO’s re-election. These uncertainties have prompted investors to express doubts about Petrobras’ future trajectory.

  • Li-Cycle surges to YTD high after securing $75B investment from Glencore

  • Chevron faces credibility crisis as Exxon plays hardball for Hess’ Guyana assets

  • Albemarle prices $2B equity offering

  • Rio Tinto, BHP downgraded at Liberum on bearish iron ore outlook
