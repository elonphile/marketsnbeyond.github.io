Markets Recap - US Stocks Hit New Highs, European Equities Soar, and Asian Markets Reach Record Peaks

Summary

  • The US stock market saw a positive week with major indexes reaching new highs on strong corporate earnings, notably from NVIDIA. Meanwhile, the bond market displayed mixed movements, influenced by hawkish remarks from the Federal Reserve and robust economic data.

  • European stocks surged to record highs, driven by strong performances from companies like NVIDIA, while government bond yields experienced a slight decrese as investors adjusted their expectations for interest rate cuts.

  • Japanese equities surged to record highs, fueled by optimism around tech firms and strong corporate profits, while Chinese markets rallied on hopes of economic recovery post-Lunar New Year, buoyed by robust tourism revenue and policy support for growth.


US Market :

The U.S. equity market saw gains throughout the shortened trading week, buoyed by robust earnings reports and positive economic data. Major indices reached new highs, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite both hitting intraday records. Notably, NVIDIA’s strong quarterly performance drove significant market excitement, contributing to the Nasdaq’s notable daily gain.


In the bond market, yields on the 10-year Treasury note hovered around 4.3%, reflecting hawkish sentiments from the Federal Reserve amid strong economic indicators. Governor Christopher Waller’s remarks highlighted the Fed’s focus on inflation and economic resilience. High yield bonds also saw support from the equity rally spurred by NVIDIA’s earnings.


Economic Data

  • Initial unemployment claims surprised analysts by dropping to a five-week low, signaling ongoing strength in the labor market. Continuing claims also decreased, indicating sustained improvements.

  • While the latest services PMI showed a slight pullback in business activity, the reading remained above the critical 50-mark, suggesting continued expansion in the services sector despite some moderation.

EU Market :

European stocks surged, with the STOXX Europe 600 Index reaching a record high, up 1.15% for the week. The rally was largely driven by robust earnings from NVIDIA, fueling global demand for technology shares. Major indices across Europe posted gains, with France’s CAC 40, Italy’s FTSE MIB, and Germany’s DAX all advancing. However, the UK’s FTSE 100 remained relatively flat, reflecting weakness in mining and energy sectors.


European government bond yields saw downward pressure as investors revised expectations for interest rate cuts following stronger-than-expected purchasing managers’ surveys. Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey’s remarks indicating comfort with rate cut expectations further influenced bond markets.


Economic Data

  • In the UK, the latest PMI survey revealed robust private sector activity in February, marking the quickest pace of expansion since May 2023, primarily driven by strong growth in the service sector.

  • However, in the eurozone, the provisional estimate of the HCOB composite PMI for output showed improvement, rising to an eight-month high of 48.9 but remained in contractionary territory.

  • Germany’s composite PMI for economic output continued to decline for the eighth consecutive month.

Asian Market :

Japanese equities reached a historic milestone as the Nikkei 225 Index soared to a new all-time high, driven by enthusiasm surrounding tech companies and a favorable economic outlook. Strong domestic corporate profits, robust export prospects amid a weaker yen, and the Bank of Japan’s commitment to maintaining accommodative monetary policy also contributed to the market rally.


The Chinese market experienced a robust rally fueled by hopes of economic recovery following strong holiday spending during the Lunar New Year. The Shanghai Composite Index and the blue chip CSI 300 surged, while the Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong also recorded significant gains. Data indicated a substantial increase in tourism revenue during the holiday period, surpassing pre-pandemic levels. Additionally, the People’s Bank of China announced a larger-than-expected reduction in the five-year loan prime rate, aiming to support economic growth.


Economic Data :

  • Japanese exports continued to show strength, reaching a record high in January with an 11.9% increase, signaling resilience in the face of global economic challenges. Machinery orders also saw growth, providing further positive momentum for the economy.

  • While Japan’s manufacturing PMI softened slightly, remaining below the expansionary threshold at 47.2, the services sector maintained its expansionary trend, albeit with a slight decrease in PMI from 53.1 to 52.5 in February.

  • New home prices in 70 Chinese cities experienced a modest decline of 0.3% sequentially in January, marking the seventh consecutive monthly contraction according to the statistics bureau, reflecting ongoing challenges in the real estate market.

Other Markets :

Currency Market: The dollar index stabilized around 104, rebounding from recent lows as another Federal Reserve official hinted at a potential delay in interest rate cuts. Despite the softer stance from EU monetary policy, the euro found support from positive sentiment in the stock market. Meanwhile, the Japanese yen weakened, approaching its lowest levels in over three months as investors continued to utilize yen for carry trades.


WTI crude futures experienced a decline to $76.49 per barrel, reflecting a weekly loss of over 3%. The drop followed comments from Fed Governor Christopher Waller suggesting potential delays in interest rate cuts, signaling concerns about economic growth and dampened oil demand. Additionally, the International Energy Agency’s report highlighted a slowdown in global oil demand due to the increasing shift towards renewable energy sources.


Gold prices remained above $2,020 an ounce, poised to end the week higher. The precious metal was supported by a weaker dollar and increased safe-haven demand amid uncertainty surrounding the timing and extent of potential interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve in response to mixed US economic data.
