Markets Recap - U.S. Hits Records, Europe Rises on Earnings, Mixed Trends in Asia

Summary

  • U.S. stocks surged to record highs for positive earning, while the bond market showed limited movement amid mixed economic data and minimal commentary from Federal Reserve officials. European indexes ended higher for the encouraging corporate results. Chinese stock market was supported by the strong stimulus measures.

  • Asian markets experienced mixed fortunes as Japan’s stocks declined on semiconductor sector concerns and slowing inflation, while China’s markets exceeded expectations with gains fueled by supportive measures and regulatory easing.

  • Other markets showed varied trends, with the dollar strengthened on solid US economy, WTI crude rising amid positive economic signals and Middle East tensions, and gold stabilizing around current levels as upbeat US economic data raised uncertainty about the Fed’s rate direction.


US Market :

In the U.S. stock market, the week witnessed continued gains for this earning reports season, with both the Dow Jones and S&P 500 reaching new all-time highs, rising by 0.65% and 1.06%, respectively. Despite the absence of notable comments from Federal Reserve officials, investors closely followed fourth-quarter earnings reports. Tesla faced a sharp decline after missing earnings and revenue estimates, while Netflix recorded gains following an unexpected increase in subscriber additions.


The U.S. bond markets remained relatively muted as Federal Reserve officials refrained from making comments or speeches. Mixed economic data contributed to the subdued atmosphere. Notable events included positive business investment data, surpassing expectations, and a balancing effect from Thursday’s jobless claims report. Consequently, the benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury note yield saw little change for the week.


Economic Data

  • The Mid-Atlantic region reported weaker-than-expected manufacturing activity on Tuesday. However, nondefense capital goods orders excluding aircraft, considered a proxy for business investment, rose by 0.3% in December.

  • Ending the week on a positive note, personal spending in December exceeded expectations, showing a 0.7% increase.

EU Market :

Most indices closed higher on the back of positive corporate results and China’s announcement of additional stimulus measures. The European Central Bank’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged and its perceived dovish outlook also boosted stocks. Major indexes saw gains, with France’s CAC 40 rising 3.56%, Germany’s DAX advancing 2.45%, Italy’s FTSE MIB gaining 0.21%, and the UK’s FTSE 100 adding 2.32%.


The EU bond market witnessed a unchanged in yields, with the 10-year German bond, along with UK sovereign bonds of the same maturity. The ECB, while maintaining its key interest rates at record highs, conveyed a commitment to sufficiently restrictive monetary policy to achieve the 2% inflation target. However, there was a perceived dovish note in acknowledging the ongoing disinflation process.


Economic Data

  • Eurozone business activity contracted for the eighth consecutive month in January, albeit at a slower rate, indicating a potential stabilization in the ongoing economic downturn.

  • The preliminary estimate of HCOB’s eurozone composite Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) showed an increase to 47.9 from 47.6 in December.

  • In the UK, business activity surpassed expectations in January, offering a positive signal for the economy’s resilience against recessionary concerns.

Asian Market :

In Japan, stock markets experienced a decline first time in the past few weeks, with the Nikkei 225 falling 0.26% and the TOPIX Index down 0.5%, largely influenced by losses in US futures and concerns about Intel’s weak guidance impacting semiconductor shares. Tokyo’s core inflation rate slowing below the Bank of Japan’s 2% target added to the market sentiment. While the BoJ maintained its ultra-accommodative stance, Governor Kazuo Ueda’s comments hinted at progress towards sustained inflation, sparking speculation about potential shifts in monetary policy.


For China, stock markets exceeded expectations as equities advanced on forceful measures from Beijing to support the economy,which really ended the eighth weeks’ loss. The Shanghai Composite Index rose 2.75%, CSI 300 gained 1.96%, and Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong advanced 4.2%. The People’s Bank of China announced a 50 basis points cut in reserve ratio requirements for most banks, signaling supportive monetary measures. Additionally, the removal of draft rules on online video games alleviated concerns and contributed to positive market sentiment.


Economic Data :


  • The Bank of Japan, in its January meeting, maintained key interest rates and committed to its yield curve control policy.

  • In Japan, spring “shunto” negotiations commenced to set nationwide wages for the fiscal year, raising expectations for substantial pay increases.

Other Markets :

In the currency market, the dollar index ended a little higher to 103.3, influenced by softer-than-expected annual core PCE inflation in the US and positive personal spending data, supporting the notion of a soft landing scenario. Despite positive US economic growth, the dollar’s biggest selling activity was noted against the euro, pound, and Australian dollar, driven by a more dovish stance signaled by the ECB, which left key interest rates unchanged.


WTI crude futures surged to $78 per barrel, marking a second consecutive week of gains and reaching a nearly two-month high. This rise was attributed to positive US economic growth, indications of Chinese stimulus boosting demand expectations, and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. US crude inventories witnessed a significant drop of 9.2 million barrels, surpassing market expectations and intensifying concerns in the oil-producing region.


Gold hovered around $2,020 an ounce as investors assessed the latest economic data from the US. Despite the resilient US economy growing at an annualized rate of 3.3% in Q4, exceeding forecasts, the upbeat figures posed a potential downward pressure on gold amid speculation about the Fed’s interest rate trajectory.
