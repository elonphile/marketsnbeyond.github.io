Markets Recap - US Tech-Driven Resilience, EU Rate Speculation, and Diverging Trends in Asia

Summary

  • US stocks remained resilient, driven by tech gains, while bond markets fluctuated around expectations of softened financial conditions. In the EU, stocks saw little change amid rate speculation, and German bonds reacted to ECB signals. Asian markets diverged, with Japan’s stocks surging and China facing challenges.

  • Forex markets indicated a rate cut probability in the U.S. higher than the Eurozone’s, stabilizing the euro. Oil prices fluctuated due to Saudi cuts and geopolitical concerns, and cryptocurrencies experienced volatility tied to ETF news.


US Market :

US stocks saw upward momentum throughout the week notably led by large-cap growth stocks and the Nasdaq Composite Index, which gained 3.09%. Energy stocks, however, underperformed as early-week pullbacks in oil prices impacted the sector. The week marked the unofficial start of earnings season, featuring reports from major banks—JPMorgan Chase, Citigroup, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo—revealing their fourth-quarter results on Friday. Tesla faced a 3.6% decline following the announcement of a production halt in its Berlin plant due to conflicts in the Red Sea, prompting shifts in transport routes. The upcoming closure of markets on Monday for Martin Luther King, Jr. Day added a note of anticipation.


In the bond markets, the yield on the 10-year US Treasury note edged towards 3.97% retracting from a three-week high of 4.04% on January 5th. This adjustment aligns with expectations of policymakers softening financial conditions in response to evidence of slowing inflation, as indicated by funds futures pricing.


Economic Data

  • December headline prices rose by 0.3%, slightly exceeding expectations, while core costs (excluding food and energy) also increased by 0.3%, aligning with consensus.

  • Headline wholesale prices experienced a third consecutive monthly decline, falling by 0.1% in December.

EU Market :

Stock markets ended gains becuase of the dovish stance from ECB. Pan-European STOXX Europe 600 Index concluded the week with minimal changes, reflecting traders’ assessments of the potential for prolonged higher interest rates. Major stock indexes presented a mixed picture, influenced notably by a dovish stance from ECB policymakers. Germany’s DAX recorded a gain of 0.66%, France’s CAC 40 Index inched up by 0.06%, and Italy’s FTSE MIB showed modest upward movement.

The bond market in Europe experienced volatility particularly following an interview on Thursday where ECB President Christine Lagarde suggested that the most challenging phase of inflation might be behind, hinting at potential interest rate cuts if inflation falls to the 2% level. This led to fluctuations in yields, with the German 10-year Bund yield retracting below 2.2%. The UK’s 10-year Gilt yield eased back to 3.8% after Friday’s economic data, with market indicators hinting at a one-third chance of an ECB rate cut as early as March.


Economic Data

  • On Friday, the UK economy exhibited resilience, growing by 0.3% in November, buoyed by strong performances in services and industrial sectors.

  • Despite an economic slowdown, the labor market remained robust, with the euro area unemployment rate standing at 6.4% in November, a slight improvement from 6.5% in October.

  • In Germany, industrial output unexpectedly declined by 0.7% sequentially in November, attributed in part to shrinking orders in the manufacturing sector.

Asian Market :

The Japanese stock market witnessed a notable surge this week, with the Nikkei 225 Index soaring by 1.15%, closing at an impressive 35,577, and the broader Topix Index climbing 0.46% to 2,494 on Friday. These levels marked their highest in almost 34 years, propelled by a weak yen and the Bank of Japan’s dovish monetary policy stance. The BoJ reiterated its commitment to maintaining an ultra-accommodative policy until sustainable inflation, driven by wage growth, materializes. The 10-year Japanese government bond yield struggled to find clear direction, fluctuating around 0.6% throughout the week.

Persistent concerns about the economy continued to weigh on the stock market. The Shanghai Composite Index declined by 1.61%, and the blue-chip CSI 300 gave up 1.35%. In Hong Kong, the benchmark Hang Seng Index fell by 1.76%, according to FactSet.

Economic Data :

  • In China, economic indicators reflected ongoing deflationary pressures, with the consumer price index falling by 0.3% in December, marking the third consecutive monthly decline.

  • The producer price index declined by 2.7% from a year ago, a slight improvement from November’s 3%, marking the 15th consecutive monthly decline. Despite a larger-than-expected trade surplus last month due to robust exports, economic challenges persist.

  • Chinese officials announced new sanctions on five U.S. defense companies in response to the latest U.S. weapons sales to Taiwan, adding a layer of complexity to the regional landscape.

Other Markets :

US dollar a bit weaker than Euro but strengted than yen this week. In the forex arena, hotter-than-expected inflation data in the U.S. and robust wholesale data presented a dilemma for the Federal Reserve. Despite the market pricing in an 80% chance of a rate cut in March, the Eurozone, with a more conservative 30% probability, is leaning towards holding rates higher for a longer duration. The euro stabilized above $1.09 with slight gains, reflecting a nuanced market sentiment. Concurrently, the yen lingered at its lowest levels in around a month, weakening beyond JPY 145 against the U.S. dollar, influenced by the Bank of Japan’s commitment to maintaining its current monetary policy stance.

Oil prices exhibited volatility throughout the week. A more than 2% decline on Monday resulted from sharp price cuts by top exporter Saudi Arabia and increased OPEC output. However, a Friday surge of over 1% followed concerns of potential supply disruptions due to U.S. and British air and sea strikes on Houthi targets in Yemen. These geopolitical developments underscored the delicate balance influencing oil market dynamics.

Cryptocurrency markets experienced heightened volatility, driven by a mix of fake news regarding ETF rejections and real approvals that supported the market. Ethereum saw notable gains, surging over 10% weekly. The contrast between misinformation and genuine regulatory developments continued to shape the trajectory of cryptocurrency prices.
