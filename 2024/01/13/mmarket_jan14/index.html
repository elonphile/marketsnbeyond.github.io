Regional Markets - Resilience and Challenges Across Brazil, Mexico, India, Russia, and Turkey

Summary

  • Brazil and Mexico exhibited distinct paths. Brazil’s real strengthened on the back of a record trade surplus and hawkish signals, while Mexico’s peso appreciated amid slightly exceeded inflation expectations and positive growth projections.

  • India’s rupee marked a four-month high, supported by robust GDP growth and central bank interventions. Russia’s ruble strengthened past 89 per USD due to central bank actions and rising oil prices from geopolitical events. In contrast, Turkey faced economic challenges, with the lira hitting a record low of 30.1 per USD, triggered by central bank signals, surging inflation, and a substantial minimum wage increase.


Latin America :

Brazil

Brazil’s resilient real amid trade surplus despite economic challenges. In Brazil, the recent PMI reading for December pointed to a contraction, primarily fueled by ongoing declines in operating conditions and heightened cost pressures. Headline inflation, standing at 4.62% in December, surpassed market expectations of 4.54%. While the central bank is anticipated to implement its fifth consecutive 50bps cut this month, the persistent risk of upside price growth may prompt policymakers to temper their dovish signals, leading to a potentially more cautious approach to Brazilian disinflation. This shift in expectations toward a more hawkish stance was evident in the currency market, where the real appreciated by 0.42% against the US dollar this week. Additionally, for the long term, Brazil’s currency exhibited resilience, attributed to the country’s record trade surplus of $98.8 billion in 2023, marking a substantial 60.6% increase from the previous year. This surplus, driven by expanded exports in agriculture and extractive industries, has not only bolstered foreign currency inflows but also contributed to the strength of the real. As Brazil navigates these economic challenges, policymakers face a delicate balance in responding to both contractionary forces and inflationary pressures.


The Ibovespa, however, experienced a 0.7% loss, initially influenced by lower oil prices earlier in the week. The later disruption of commercial routes in the Red Sea due to U.S. and UK airstrikes on Houthi targets in Yemen led to a surge in oil prices by week-end, benefitting Petrobras and ultimately contributing to a 0.3% increase in the Ibovespa.


Mexico

In Mexico, mirroring the trend in Brazil, the Mexican peso strengthened, surpassing the 16.86 per USD level. December’s inflation data revealed a month-over-month headline inflation of 0.7%, with a year-over-year rate of 4.7%, slightly exceeding expectations. The central bank is anticipated to maintain a vigilant stance against broader inflation, signaling a reluctance for immediate interest rate cuts until sustained signs of longer-lasting disinflation emerge. Concurrently, the World Bank adjusted Mexico’s growth projection for 2024, elevating it from 1.9% to a more optimistic 2.6%. This adjustment underscores the resilience of the Mexican economy in the face of aggressive rate hikes in the previous year, providing a positive outlook for the nation’s economic trajectory.

Recent economic indicators further underscored the resilience of the Mexican economy, with business confidence reaching a ten-year high. Additionally, unemployment remained at levels unseen in over twenty years. These factors collectively contribute to a favorable outlook for Mexico’s economic stability and growth.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increased by 0.07% on Friday for the week. Notably, Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, declined 2.11% this week.

Asia :

India

Rupee continued its momentum. In India, the momentum of the Indian rupee continued, appreciating beyond 83 per USD, marking its strongest position in four months. This strengthening trend is attributed to robust economic data and sustained intervention by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). India’s GDP growth stood at an impressive 7.3% in the 2023-24 fiscal year, surpassing market expectations and highlighting the resilience of the Indian economy amidst the RBI’s tightening cycle in the preceding year. The RBI’s consistent intervention in the foreign exchange markets, preventing the rupee from breaching its record low of 83.4, has instilled confidence in investors. This proactive approach by the central bank has contributed to the rupee’s positive trajectory, signaling a favorable outlook for India’s economic stability.


The S&P BSE Sensex climbed 1.2% to close at an all-time high of 72,568 points on Friday, and the NSE Nifty 50 surged by 1.1% to end at a record 21,895 points, led by increases in IT companies, supported by positive quarterly reports from both Tata Consultancy Services and Infosys.

EU :

Russia

Russian currency and index react positively to the geopolitical tension. The Russian ruble crossed the 89 per USD mark, reaching its highest level since November’s end, propelled by intensified interventions by the central bank and a surge in oil prices following U.S. and UK missile strikes in Yemen. Additionally, the Russian finance ministry announced a shift from forex purchases to sales in the coming weeks, aiming to offset lower revenues from oil and gas in the past month. Higher oil prices, further boosted by Western strikes on Houthi military targets in Yemen, contributed to the positive momentum, reflecting the intricate interplay of global events impacting Russia’s currency and economic indicators.


Turkey

The Turkish lira continued its downward spiral reaching an unprecedented low of 30.1 per USD, prompted by remarks from central bank chief Erkan signaling an early end to the monetary policy tightening cycle. Despite Turkey’s annual inflation soaring to an alarming 64.77% in December, with expectations of a continued upward trajectory in the coming months, Erkan’s comments added to concerns. The substantial increase in the minimum wage, a contributing factor to inflation, aimed at alleviating living costs ahead of the upcoming municipal elections in March. With the net minimum wage for 2024 raised to 17,002 liras ($578), the Turkish lira has witnessed a staggering decline of over a third in the past year and more than 80% over the last five years, reflecting the deepening economic challenges facing Turkey.
