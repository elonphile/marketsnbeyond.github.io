Markets Recap - Navigate Earnings Impact, Central Bank Signals

Summary

  • U.S. markets fluctuated on early earnings impact, tech resilience lifted stocks, bond rates stabilized on FED signals.European stocks faced declines amid a hawkish ECB stance and mixed economic signals.

  • Japan’s stock market surged on a weakening yen and positive inflation data, while China faced unexpected declines due to weak economic indicators.

  • The dollar strengthened, buoyed by robust US data and a hawkish Fed, impacting the yen, which weakened as Finance Minister Suzuki emphasized stable currency moves; oil prices held gains amid Middle East tensions and positive demand outlook, while gold declined on strengthened dollar and Treasury yields.


US Market :

The U.S. stock market witnessed volatility as the fourth-quarter earnings season unfolded. Boeing’s earnings report, coupled with delivery concerns for its 737 MAX airliners, initially led to declines. However, a recovery ensued, with the S&P 500 closing at an all-time high of 4,839, driven by information technology stocks. Semiconductor shares, fueled by TSM’s positive earnings, boosted tech giants like Nvidia, a 4.2% increase, Advanced Micro Devices, and Texas Instruments.


In the U.S. bond markets, expectations for 2024 rate cuts sharply declined. The likelihood of a March rate cut dropped from 81.0% to 47.4%, supported by robust economic data and a signal from the Fed governor. Jobless claims fell to 187K, indicating a tight labor market, while December retail activity surpassed expectations at 0.6%. FOMC member Waller opposed rate cuts, citing a strong labor market and economic activity.


Economic Data

  • A dip in sentiment due to a manufacturing activity index plunge in the New York region.

  • Friday saw a positive turn with the University of Michigan’s consumer sentiment index reaching its highest level in nearly three years, marking the most significant increase since 2005.

EU Market :

European markets faced declines as the ECB adopted a hawkish stance, responding to weakened economic data. German producer prices plummeted more than anticipated in December, showcasing an 8.6% year-on-year decrease. The pan-European STOXX Europe 600 Index ended the week 1.58% lower as comments from central bank policymakers prompted financial markets to scale back bets on an early reduction in interest rates. Major stock indexes were mainly softer. France’s CAC 40 Index fell 1.25%, Germany’s DAX declined 0.89%, Italy’s FTSE MIB eased 0.61%, and the UK’s FTSE 100 Index lost 2.14%.


The EU bond market, responding to the ECB’s cautious outlook, witnessed a surge in yields, particularly evident in the UK. ECB President Christine Lagarde’s signal that interest rate cuts might occur in the summer, contrary to market expectations, contributed to the evolving financial landscape.


Economic Data

  • In Germany, the significant dip in producer prices underscored economic challenges.

  • UK experienced an unexpected uptick in annual inflation to 4.0% in December, marking the first increase in ten months.

  • Retail sales volumes, which plummeted by 3.2% sequentially in December, recording the most substantial month-over-month drop since January 2021.

Asian Market :

In the Asian markets, Japan’s stock market surged as the yen weakened and inflation data exceeded expectations. The Nikkei 225 Index reached a 34-year high, rising 1.1%, driven by robust performance from exporters. However, Japan’s bond market saw upward pressure on yields due to a more hawkish tone from US Federal Reserve officials. The 10-year Japanese government bond yield rose to 0.66%. Becuase of signs of easing inflationary pressure, the Bank of Japan is more likelt to maintain its monetary policy stance.


Persistent concerns about the economy continued to weigh on the stock market. Economic indicators painted a bleak outlook, leading to concerns about the country’s economic trajectory. In the bond market, the People’s Bank of China injected an above-forecast RMB 995 billion to boost demand, reflecting anticipation of potential policy loosening in the coming year.


Economic Data :


  • Japan’s economic data revealed a mixed picture, with core consumer price index (CPI) slowing to 2.3% in December, signaling a predicted slowdown in consumer inflation.

  • Retail sales in China, growing 7.4% in December, fell short of expectations, while fixed-asset investment exceeded forecasts at 3.0% for the full year, driven by infrastructure growth.

  • A deepening decline in real estate investment posed challenges. December industrial production surpassed expectations, but urban unemployment inched up to 5.1% from November’s 5.0%, adding complexities to the economic narrative.

  • China’s new home prices fell 0.4% in December, down from November’s 0.3% decline

Other Markets :

In the forex market, the dollar index held steady around 103.3, securing a weekly gain propelled by robust US data and a hawkish stance from Federal Reserve officials, diminishing expectations of a March interest rate cut. The yen weakened against the dollar, reaching JPY 148, prompting Finance Minister Shunichi Suzuki to emphasize the government’s vigilance on currency moves.


Oil prices experienced slight declines but maintained a weekly gain, with WTI crude futures at approximately $73.7 per barrel. Escalating Middle East tensions and a positive global oil demand outlook supported the market. The US responded to Houthi attacks with strikes in Yemen, and the US EIA reported a larger-than-expected 2.5 million barrel decline in crude inventories.


In contrast, gold faced declines as the dollar and Treasury yields strengthened, propelled by solid US data and a hawkish Federal Reserve, mitigating expectations of interest rate cuts. The dynamics in the forex and commodity markets reflect the intricate balance between geopolitical tensions, economic indicators, and central bank messaging.at supported the market.
