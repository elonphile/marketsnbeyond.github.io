Stocks Recap - Japanese Carmakers Excel, Auto Sector Challenges, TSM and ASML Lead

Summary

  • For Carmakers, Japanese stocks, led by Toyota’s 2.87% gains, outperformed as the US Federal Reserve’s firm stance favored the market. Challenges persist for the automotive sector, while the upcoming election could reshape policies

  • TSM and ASML, prominent EV suppliers in this week’s portfolio, witnessed substantial gains, particularly TSM, whose Q4 data surpassed expectations with a revenue of $20.10 billion, signaling an optimistic 2024 outlook driven by recovering chip demand and strategic expansions.


CARMAKER :


This week witnessed diverse path in EV sector. Japanese stocks, led by Toyota’s 2.87% gains, performed well this week due to the US Federal Reserve’s firm stance reducing the likelihood of an interest rate cut in March. The resulting strengthening of the US dollar benefited Japan’s export industry. On the flip side, Chinese carmakers, such as NIO and XPENG, experienced a notable decline, possibly linked to news of additional electric vehicle (EV) price cuts in Asia and Europe.


In the US, the automotive sector, particularly EV companies like Rivian, faced challenges, evidenced by Rivian’s eighth consecutive session of losses and a Deutsche Bank downgrade from Buy on concerns over near-term volume and margins.


The upcoming US presidential election is crucial for investors, as changes in the political landscape can impact environmental policies, regulatory frameworks, and trade negotiations. The potential disruption in federal support for the EV transition is a key concern, especially if a Republican-led administration revisits existing laws and funding.


The US election impact on companies varies; Tesla may face challenges due to potential policy changes affecting its Mexican plants, while Chinese carmakers could benefit from incentives and EV-related tax adjustments. Additionally, a GOP-led administration may roll back climate-friendly policies, favoring companies like Ford with slower transition strategies, including hybrids and plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) solutions.

HIDDEN CHAMPION :


TSM and ASML emerged as top performers among EV suppliers this week. In this week’s portfolio, TSM (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) and ASML stand out among EV suppliers, showcasing impressive gains of 12% and 6% respectively. TSM’s Q4 data surpassed expectations, reporting a robust fourth-quarter revenue of $20.10 billion, as calculated by Reuters.


Looking ahead to TSMC’s 2024 outlook, there is optimism fueled by the anticipated recovery in chip demand. This resurgence is attributed to smartphone and PC manufacturers seeking to replenish their diminishing inventories. TSMC’s revenue growth in 2024 is expected to be driven by the ramp-up of 3nm technology, expansion in CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) technology, and the overall recovery in demand. Additionally, TSMC aims to maintain a solid market share, contributing to its positive outlook.
