Regional Markets - Impact of US Inflation Data on Emerging Markets

Summary

  • Latin American currencies showed similar performance for weak dollar, with the Mexican peso strengthening on positive economic data while the Brazilian real stabilized amid hawkish policy expectations.

  • US inflation data similarily impacted other markets, leading to appreciation in the Indian rupee, Russian ruble and Turkish lira but for silght different reason including stabilizing amid tight monetary policy and improved trade data for Russia, positive economic restructuring sentiment and credit rating upgrades for Turkey.


Latin America :

Brazil

The Brazilian real stabilized around 5.10 per USD, supported by economic inflation data from the US and expectations for a rate cut. The Brazilian central bank’s hawkish stance added further upside pressure. With the annual inflation rate dropping to 3.69% in April, closely aligned with forecasts and nearing the central bank’s 3.5% target, the BCB gained more flexibility for a hawkish policy approach. The Ibovespa, Brazil’s main stock market index, witnessed weekly gains of 0.4%.



Mexico

Similarly, the Mexican peso strengthened, surpassing 16.8 per USD to its highest level since mid-April, buoyed by weakness in the US dollar and positive domestic economic indicators. April’s Mexican Core CPI saw a modest increase of 0.21% MoM, slightly below expectations and significantly lower than the previous month’s rise of 0.44%. Additionally, the US dollar faced downward pressure amid concerns about a weakening labor market, prompting expectations for looser borrowing conditions by the Fed Reserve in June. The IPC, Mexico’s main stock market index, has gained 0.34% since the beginning of 2024.


Argentina

The USD to ARS exchange rate increase by 0.47% for the week. Argentina’s main stock market index, Merval, surged 6.71% this week.

Asia :

India

The Indian rupee strengthened, surpassing 83.29 per USD, marking its highest level in over three weeks, finding relief amidst increased expectations of a Fed rate cut by September, which alleviated pressure from a strong US dollar. Furthermore, anticipation of rate cuts by the PBoC and continued weakness in the yen intensified selling pressure for riskier currencies across Asia. The BSE Sensex climbed 253 points, or 0.3%, to close at 73,917 on Friday, reaching its highest point in over two weeks and marking the second consecutive session of gains. Notably, Mahindra & Mahindra experienced a significant surge of nearly 6% in its share price, supported by robust financial results for the March quarter and optimistic outlook commentary from brokerage firms.

EU :

Russia

The Russian ruble stabilized near the 90.95 per USD mark, marking its strongest position in two months, supported by the Central Bank of Russia’s (CBR) tight monetary policy stance and positive trade figures. With the CBR signaling that monetary easing is distant and not ruling out further rate hikes for the year, investor sentiment was bolstered. Additionally, increased foreign exchange inflows from key countries’ oil and gas shipments, coupled with declining imports, contributed to the ruble’s resilience. The ruble-based MOEX Russia index climbed to 3,504 on Friday, as investors awaited preliminary GDP results and monthly inflation data after the closing bell, while also monitoring events from the second day of Putin’s visit to China.


Turkey

The Turkish lira saw a slight strengthening to the 32.16 per USD level, rebounding from its record low of 32.6 in the previous week of April, amidst growing optimism surrounding Turkey’s economic rebalancing efforts. Notably, credit rating agency S&P upgraded Turkey’s rating to “B+” from “B”, citing improvements in monetary, fiscal, and income policy coordination. Despite an annual inflation rate that rose less-than-expected to 69.80% in April, the BIST 100 index surged to a new all-time high of 10,500. This rally is attributed to high inflation eroding consumers’ savings, prompting retail investors to seek refuge in the stock market to preserve their wealth.
