Markets Recap - US Hits Record Highs, Europe Wavers on ECB Caution, Asian Sentiment Mixed

Summary

  • US markets rallied to record highs as inflation concerns eased with the latest CPI data, while European markets initially surged but later retreated on cautious comments from ECB members regarding monetary policy easing, particularly in light of strong UK pay growth potentially fueling inflationary pressures.

  • Asian markets saw Japanese stocks rise on the back of a stronger yen, while Chinese markets remained cautious despite government intervention to stabilize the property sector.

  • In other markets, the dollar depreciated amid expectations of rate cuts, while WTI crude futures strengthened on declining inventories and optimism about potential Fed rate cuts, and gold prices surged to a record high fueled by expectations of Fed easing and geopolitical tensions.


US Market :

US stock markets climbed to record highs this week, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average crossing the 40,000 threshold for the first time. Despite ongoing concerns about interest rates, Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell reiterated the central bank’s commitment to achieving its inflation target. Inflationary pressures appeared to ease, benefiting growth stocks as lower implied discount rates improved the outlook for future earnings. The Labor Department’s April consumer price index (CPI) report, released on Wednesday, showed headline prices rising 0.3%, slightly below expectations, while core prices (excluding food and energy) increased by 0.3%, in line with forecasts. This was a contrast to the hotter-than-expected inflation readings from the previous three months.


The bond markets responded to the growth and inflation news, with the yield on the benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury note falling to its lowest level in over a month by midweek.


Economic Data

  • Headline prices rose 0.3% in April, a tick below expectations, while core (less food and energy) prices rose 0.3%, as expected

  • The Commerce Department reported that retail sales were flat in April versus consensus estimates of a 0.4% gain

EU Market :

European stock markets mirrored movements in the US, initially reaching record highs but later slipping due to cautious comments from European Central Bank (ECB) members that tempered optimism about potential monetary easing this year. Major stock indexes were mixed: Germany’s DAX fell 0.36%, France’s CAC 40 Index declined 0.63%, while Italy’s FTSE MIB advanced 2.14%. The UK’s FTSE 100 Index finished modestly lower, partly due to concerns over strong pay growth potentially leading to persistent inflation. The annual rate of pay growth, excluding bonuses, remained unchanged at 6% for the three months through March, slightly above forecasts.


In the bond markets, yields were mixed. The data tentatively support a potential reduction in interest rates in June, as there are signs of a transition to a looser labor market. However, policymakers emphasized the need for additional data before making a final decision on rate cuts. ECB policymakers, including Francois Villeroy de Galhau, Madis Muller, and Martins Kazaks, indicated that while a rate cut in June is likely, the future path remains uncertain.


Economic Data

  • Unemployment rate in France stays at 7.5% in Q1

  • France inflation rate maintained at 2.2% in April

  • Spain’s inflation rate rises to 3.3% in April

  • Germany’s inflation stays at 2.2%, meeting consensus

  • UK’s unemployment rate climbs to 4.3% in March

Asian Market :

Japanese stock markets closed the week higher, supported by the appreciation of the yen, with the Nikkei 225 Index gaining 1.46% and the TOPIX Index up 0.6%. The yen remained range-bound amid expectations of US interest rate cuts, contrasting with the tentative hawkishness of the Bank of Japan (BoJ), which modestly lifted Japanese government bond (JGB) yields.


In China, stock markets saw little change due to cautious sentiment surrounding the economy, despite a historic rescue package from the central government to stabilize the property sector. The Shanghai Composite Index remained flat, while the CSI 300 added 0.32%. The People’s Bank of China (PBOC) attempted to stimulate demand by lowering the minimum down payment ratio for homebuyers, but China’s housing crisis persisted, with new home prices falling for the 10th consecutive month. Deflationary pressures continued to weigh on the economy, with the producer price index declining by 2.5% year-on-year in April.


Economic Data :

  • China announces major rescue measures for housing market

  • China’s industrial production in April grows more than anticipated; retail sales rise less than forecast

  • Japan Q1 GDP shrinks more than expected on weak consumer spending

Other Markets :

In the currency markets, the dollar depreciated against major currencies due to expectations of rate cuts by major economies. The yen remained broadly unchanged against the USD, reflecting expectations of potential rate cuts by the U.S. Federal Reserve and further monetary policy normalization by the Bank of Japan, narrowing the interest rate differential between the two economies.


WTI crude futures strengthened above $79 per barrel, supported by recent declines in US crude oil inventories and growing optimism surrounding potential interest rate cuts by the US Federal Reserve. EIA data indicating consecutive weekly declines in crude stockpiles surpassed forecasts, further boosting oil prices. EIA data showed that US crude stockpiles fell by 2.508 million barrels last week, declining for the second straight week and exceeding forecasts for a 1.362 million barrel draw.


Gold prices surged past $2,400 per ounce, reaching a fresh record high, driven by increased expectations of Federal Reserve interest rate cuts, central bank purchases, and safe-haven demand amid heightened geopolitical tensions. Signs of a slowdown in US consumer inflation and stagnant retail sales have provided the Fed with more flexibility to initiate monetary easing, enhancing the appeal of non-bearing assets like gold.
